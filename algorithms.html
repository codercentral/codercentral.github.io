Introduction to Algorithmic Computing
Algorithms are very important in almost every field of computing. In this course, you will learn how to use algorithms to solve complex problems, and develop the critical thinking skills necessary for algorithmic computing. This course, taught by Jerry Li, a Computer Science major at UCLA, is perfect for anyone who is looking to dive deeper into programming and our robotics track or participate in coding competitions like the USA Computing Olympiad. Although we will be using the C++ programming language, anyone who already mastered the fundamentals of any language can also take this course and will be able to keep up.

Course Overview

1 Introduction, C++ Syntax, and Basics Review
2 Sorting and Searching
3 Advanced Sorting Algorithms and Algorithmic Complexity
4 Stacks, Queues, and Vectors
5 Review, Practice, and In-Class Project
6 Recursion
7 Trees and Graphs
8 Flood Fill and Shortest Paths
9 Review and Practice Problems
10 Mock Coding Competition!

Instructor

Jerry Li is a current Computer Science and Engineering major at UCLA, who has been teaching computer science and mathematics to students of all grade levels for many years. He has participated in numerous programming and mathematics competitions in the past and has won a number of awards, including qualification for the USA Computing Olympiad Silver Division (top ~400 in the US). Jerry has successfully taught several computer science and competitive mathematics courses for middle and high school students in the past few years. In his free time, he likes to play badminton, watch anime, and play board games.
