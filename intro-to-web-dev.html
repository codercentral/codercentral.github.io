Intro to Web Development - Course Overview

June 13 - June 17, 4:00pm - 6:00pm | San Ramon, CA

Web Design

Students will learn how to build their own website using HTML and CSS. HTML allows students to create their webpage, while CSS will allow them to design the page and utilize their creative skills. We will also be teaching them how to use basic JavaScript -- a powerful programming language used primarily for scripting in web development.

No Experience Necessary


All curriculum for this bootcamp are designed for beginners. Students do not need prior experience in programming to join the class. Students should simply bring their creativity, motivation, and desire to learn!

Personalized education


Our bootcamp, designed for 6th - 12th graders, simulate the format of college courses. Lessons are taught by the instructor to the whole class, and smaller workshop sections led by TA's will clarify and work through any questions, and facilitate brainstorming, problem solving, and designing sessions.

Project Based Curriculum


We emphasize having students learn through hands-on projects and problem sets. By the end of the class, they will have created their own website with animations.
